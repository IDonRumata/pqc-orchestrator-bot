HORIZON EUROPE - CYBERSECURITY AND PQC FUNDING OPPORTUNITIES
EU Framework Programme for Research and Innovation (2021-2027)
Total budget: 95.5 billion EUR
Managed by: European Commission, CINEA, EIC, ENISA (co-funded calls)
Main portal: https://eic.ec.europa.eu / https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/

=== PROGRAMME STRUCTURE RELEVANT TO PQC ===

Pillar I: Excellent Science
- European Research Council (ERC): fundamental research grants
- Marie Sklodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA): researcher mobility

Pillar II: Global Challenges and European Industrial Competitiveness
- Cluster 3: Civil Security for Society (directly relevant to PQC)
  * Budget 2021-2027: ~1.6 billion EUR
  * Covers: cybersecurity, digital privacy, trust technologies
  * Post-quantum cryptography is explicitly listed as priority area
  * NIS2/DORA compliance tools are eligible for Cluster 3 funding

Pillar III: Innovative Europe
- European Innovation Council (EIC): flagship for startups
- European Innovation Ecosystems (EIE)

=== EIC ACCELERATOR - PRIMARY INSTRUMENT FOR PQC STARTUPS ===

EIC Accelerator: https://eic.ec.europa.eu/eic-funding-opportunities/eic-accelerator_en

Type: Non-dilutive grant + optional equity investment
Grant component: up to EUR 2.5 million (non-dilutive) - covers R&D and innovation
Equity component: up to EUR 15 million (EIC Fund takes equity stake, optional)
Blended finance: grant + equity combined up to EUR 17.5 million total

Eligibility:
- SMEs (fewer than 250 employees, under EUR 50M turnover or EUR 43M balance sheet)
- Established in EU Member States or associated countries
- Innovative technology with market potential
- "Deep tech" focus preferred: cybersecurity, PQC, quantum technologies qualify

Application requirements:
- Step 1: Short application (online form, ~10 pages, pitch video)
- Step 2: Full proposal (detailed plan, team CVs, financials, market analysis)
- Step 3: Interview with jury panel

Key selection criteria:
- Excellence and innovation level (breakthrough potential)
- Impact (market size, scalability, societal value)
- Quality and maturity of the team
- Technology readiness level (TRL 5+ preferred for Accelerator)

PQC startup fit for EIC Accelerator:
- HNDL threat = "imminent market need" (strong impact case)
- NIST FIPS 203/204/205 = "validated technology" (standardization reduces risk)
- EU regulatory push (NIS2, DORA) = "regulatory pull" (market demand driver)
- SME quantum cybersecurity market growing: projected 5.1B USD by 2030 (MarketsandMarkets)

EIC Accelerator open calls: approximately 3 cut-off dates per year
Check: https://eic.ec.europa.eu/eic-funding-opportunities/eic-accelerator_en

=== HORIZON EUROPE CLUSTER 3 - PROJECT GRANTS ===

Cluster 3 Topic: "Cybersecurity - Next Generation Internet and PQC"
Typical topics appearing in Work Programmes:
- HORIZON-CL3-2024-CS-01: Cybersecurity in the domain of NIS2 and the DORA regulation
- HORIZON-CL3-2024-CS-02: Tools supporting cybersecurity risk assessment
- HORIZON-CL3-PQC: Post-quantum cryptography migration tools (ad hoc calls)

Consortium requirement: typically 3+ partners from 3+ EU countries (or associated)
Grant rate: 70-100% (100% for non-profit research, 70% for commercial entities)
Project size: typically EUR 3-8 million for multi-partner projects

For a startup as consortium partner: can receive up to EUR 2-3 million as subcontractor/SME partner.

ENISA-JRC co-funded calls:
ENISA regularly co-designs calls with the Commission focused on:
- PQC migration tools and frameworks
- Cryptographic agility standards
- Quantum readiness assessments

=== EIC PATHFINDER - FOR EARLY-STAGE RESEARCH ===

EIC Pathfinder Open: https://eic.ec.europa.eu/eic-funding-opportunities/eic-pathfinder_en
Budget: up to EUR 4 million per project, typically EUR 2-4M
For: breakthrough research, TRL 1-4
Consortium: typically 2-6 partners, lower consortium requirements than main Horizon calls

Suitable if PQC startup is at early research stage or wants to develop novel PQC algorithms.

=== HORIZON EUROPE APPLICATION PROCESS ===

Portal: https://eic.ec.europa.eu or https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/
Steps:
1. Create EU login account at https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/
2. Register organization in Funding and Tenders Portal
3. Find open call under relevant Work Programme
4. Prepare and submit application before deadline

EU PIC (Participant Identification Code): required for all applicants
- Register free: https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/opportunities/portal/screen/how-to-participate/participant-register

Timing: typical evaluation period 3-5 months, funding start ~6 months after deadline

Budget eligibility:
- Personnel costs (salaries + overheads up to 25% flat rate)
- Subcontracting (limited)
- Travel and subsistence
- Equipment and infrastructure (amortized or full cost)
- Other direct costs
