DIGITAL EUROPE PROGRAMME (DEP) - CYBERSECURITY AND PQC
EU Programme 2021-2027, Budget: 7.6 billion EUR total
Cybersecurity component: ~1.6 billion EUR (Specific Objective 3)
Managed by: ENISA, European Cybersecurity Competence Centre (ECCC), CINEA
Main portal: https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/activities/digital-programme
ECCC portal: https://www.eccc.europa.eu/

=== PROGRAMME OVERVIEW ===

Digital Europe Programme differs from Horizon Europe:
- Horizon Europe: RESEARCH and INNOVATION (TRL 1-7, R&D)
- Digital Europe Programme: DEPLOYMENT and UPTAKE (TRL 7-9, operational)

Digital Europe cybersecurity supports:
- Deployment of existing cybersecurity technologies
- Capacity building and skills
- Procurement of cybersecurity tools for public sector
- Support for NIS2/DORA implementation

=== SPECIFIC OBJECTIVE 3 - CYBERSECURITY ===

Strategic objectives:
1. Strengthen the cybersecurity capabilities of the EU
2. Support implementation of the EU Cybersecurity Act and NIS2
3. Build cybersecurity capacity across Member States
4. Develop advanced cybersecurity tools and technologies

Post-quantum cryptography is a NAMED PRIORITY in DEP cybersecurity work programmes:
- Deployment support for quantum-safe TLS migration
- Tools for cryptographic agility assessment
- PQC migration frameworks and toolkits
- Training and awareness on quantum threats

=== ECCC (EUROPEAN CYBERSECURITY COMPETENCE CENTRE) ===

ECCC coordinates EU cybersecurity investment from Digital Europe and Horizon Europe.
Address: Budapest, Hungary
Website: https://www.eccc.europa.eu/

ECCC Work Programmes issue Calls for Proposals:
- Typically 2-4 calls per year
- SMEs can apply directly or as part of consortia
- National Coordination Centres (NCCs) support applicants in each country

For Poland - National Coordination Centre:
NASK (Naukowa i Akademicka Siec Komputerowa)
Address: Ul. Kolska 12, 01-045 Warszawa
NCC Poland: https://www.nask.pl/
CERT Polska is part of NASK and coordinates ECCC matters for Poland

=== TYPICAL CALLS UNDER DIGITAL EUROPE CYBERSECURITY ===

Call structure example (Work Programme 2023-2024):
- DIGITAL-ECCC-2024-DEPLOY-CYBER-06-TOOLS: Cybersecurity tools and frameworks
  * Eligible: companies and organizations deploying operational cybersecurity solutions
  * Grant rate: up to 50% (co-financing required)
  * Budget per project: EUR 500K - 3M
  * PQC migration tools explicitly listed as priority area

- DIGITAL-ECCC-2024-DEPLOY-CYBER-05-SKILLS: Cybersecurity skills and training
  * For: training providers, industry associations, academic institutions
  * Includes PQC-specific training programs

- DIGITAL-ECCC-2024-DEPLOY-CYBER-04-GOVERNANCE: NIS2 implementation support
  * For: national authorities and their ICT/security providers
  * Indirect market: startups can be service providers to beneficiaries

=== FINANCIAL INTERMEDIARIES AND MIXING ===

Key principle: EU grants can often be STACKED with Polish programs.
Example stack for a PQC startup:

Source 1: NCBR Sciezka SMART B+R module - covers R&D costs (up to 80%)
Source 2: EIC Accelerator grant - covers market entry and scale-up costs (up to 2.5M EUR)
Source 3: Digital Europe deployment project - covers customer-side deployment support
Source 4: BGK low-interest loan - covers operational capital between grants

The rule: you cannot finance the SAME COST from two EU sources simultaneously.
But you can finance different budget lines from different programs.

Recommended consultation: PARP provides free grant stacking advisory service.
Book via: https://www.parp.gov.pl/doradztwo-dla-msp

=== EU PQC PROCUREMENT - INDIRECT REVENUE OPPORTUNITY ===

The EU and Member States are procuring PQC-ready solutions for public systems:
- NIS2 requires public sector to deploy state-of-the-art cryptography
- EU institutions (EC, EMA, EIB, etc.) are auditing and upgrading cryptographic systems
- Estimated procurement: EUR 200-500M over 2025-2028 for EU cryptographic infrastructure

Procurement vehicles:
- PEPPOL / TED (Tenders Electronic Daily): https://ted.europa.eu/ - monitor for cybersecurity tenders
- NATO NCIA procurement for PQC (defense-adjacent)
- Polish ZKP (Zamowienia Publiczne): https://www.gov.pl/web/uzp - local public procurement

Revenue model from procurement: Audit as a Service (AaaS) contracts with public bodies
- EU institutions pay EUR 50-200K per compliance audit
- Repeat engagements as regulations update (ENISA guidelines update annually)
- Framework agreements typically 2-4 years

=== SUMMARY: GRANT PRIORITY RANKING FOR PQC STARTUP ===

Priority 1 - IMMEDIATE ACTION (months 1-3):
- Contact PARP for free advisory
- Register in EU Funding & Tenders Portal (get PIC)
- Join ECCC Community (free, enables future calls)
- Register on KPK Poland mailing list for Horizon calls

Priority 2 - SHORT TERM (months 3-9):
- Sciezka SMART via PARP (up to 20M PLN, covers 50-80% of R&D costs)
- EIC Accelerator application (up to EUR 2.5M non-dilutive + optional equity)

Priority 3 - MEDIUM TERM (months 6-18):
- Digital Europe ECCC call (deployment-focused, 50% co-financing)
- Horizon Europe Cluster 3 (research partner role, or consortium lead)
- NCBR programs with academic partner

Non-grant funding to bridge gaps:
- BGK loan (preferencyjny): low-interest, available from BGK branches
- Polish VC with public co-investor: OTB Ventures, Inovo, PKO/Startup Hub
